Thesis-level blind spot: find-first personas
Headline. The flow delivers its core thesis for the three Editor personas it was built around — recognition + contextual questions + returning-user pills make the second session genuinely feel like the tool remembered them. It is weakest at its own seams: the find-first personas (Knowledge Consumer, Q2 primary) never trigger areas 01/02/04 because all three assume authoring, and the MVP abstracts away the highest-converting behaviours (node-level AI, teammate invite) as a deliberate clean-signal trade.
01

Findings by feature area

Each area evaluated against its documented hypothesis, per persona.

Area 01 · Trigger: first prompt

Prompt-recognition analysis

Holds for authors
Hypothesis: segmenting on the first prompt makes every later surface feel tailored → lifts first-session activation.

Holds for personas who type intent — the Sensemaker's "auth / GitHub / flow" prompt visibly shapes the follow-ups and output. It's invisible plumbing, felt not seen, which is correct.

Seam   The Consumer and Maintainer don't author from cold start, so recognition has no prompt to read — areas 01/02/04 never fire for them.

Area 02 · Trigger: loading, S1 only

Contextual questions — "Let's personalize this"

Holds, watch effort
Hypothesis: diagram-aware single-select questions during creation raise output acceptance without adding perceived effort.

Diagram-aware Skip/Yes questions are exactly the "intelligent, not generic" signal intended. But the Sensemaker reads three questions as a quiz before seeing output (reaches for Skip), and the Contributor notes they're diagram-aware, not brand-aware — none asks "apply your theme?"

Area 03 · Trigger: first session, in-editor

Get-Started checklist · Create → Edit with AI → Theme → Share

Fits MVP goal
Hypothesis: a simple sequence ending on Share yields a clean engagement signal and converts better than gating on Invite. (§10 resolved — treated as fixed.)

Maps cleanly: Sensemaker gets create + AI-edit + share; the Contributor is directly served by "Create a theme" as a first-class step.

Bets to validate   Collaborator's session-1 invite intent now depends on noticing the Join-Org banner; and generic "Edit with AI" trades away node/voice (1.88% / 2.83% paid conv.) — the trial→paid metric adjudicates.

Area 04 · Trigger: 2nd / 3rd+ visit

Progressive onboarding

Strongest part
Hypothesis: behaviour-driven re-entry (not a fixed per-mode template) lifts return-visit retention — the second session proves the tool was paying attention.

Returning pills built from the last session, the @-mention teaching pattern, and per-mode flavour land as the differentiator for all three Editors.

Bonus   The Code-mode "diagram from your latest GitHub branch" tile is a partial answer to the Maintainer's freshness need — better than "no sync semantics."

04

Recommendations

Ranked, tied to the hypotheses and the trial→paid goal.

1

Instrument the two checklist bets directly

Add the missing "success" event (first accepted AI op / first export), then compare generic "Edit with AI" conversion vs deferred node/voice, and Join-Org banner invite-rate vs an in-checklist invite. This tells you whether the clean-signal trades cost trial→paid.

2

Show what Share produces

Live link vs image and embed targets (Confluence / Notion / GitHub). The shared open question across Sensemaker, Collaborator, Contributor — and Share is now the checklist's terminal step, so its payoff must be legible.

3

Confirm the Collaborator finds the Join-Org banner in session 1

The invite relocation is sound; its only risk is discoverability at peak intent.

4

Decide the find-first story explicitly

Areas 01/02/04 assume an author. For the Q2-primary Consumer, either add a "find the canonical diagram" returning state + author/canonical metadata on cards, or document that App personas are served by search elsewhere.

5

Close the Contributor's theme loop

Make "Create a theme" demonstrably save + inherit across diagrams and signal that AI generation respects it — ties to enterprise brand-default demand.

6

Leave the two parked pieces parked unless instrumented

The conversation-ordered quick-action box is the origin of the earlier "conversion stats on cards" scare — if ever designed in, use user-value labels, never raw conversion %. Keep tool-detection a quiet hint (the old popover hit 52% dismiss).

Caveats. Scored against documented hypotheses; §10 resolved decisions and §11 known placeholders treated as fixed, not flagged. Synthetic personas are a fast directional pass anchored in real customer quotes — not a substitute for live users; the most reliable signals are seams recurring across multiple personas. Maintainer and Steward are partly out of scope for a first-session activation flow.
